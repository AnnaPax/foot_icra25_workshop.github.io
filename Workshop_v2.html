Enhancing Locomotion: Robotic and Prosthetic Foot Design

Workshop at ICRA 2025 | Atlanta, USA | May 2025

About Schedule Speakers Organizers Register

ABOUT

This workshop explores the critical influence of foot design on whole-body locomotion of biped robots and the gait of prosthetic users with lower-limb loss in real-world scenarios. Replicating the locomotion efficiency provided by the human foot, with its unique anatomical structure, has been the challenging goal of robotic and prosthetic design. In the last two decades, several passive and active designs of artificial feet for robotics and prosthetics applications have been developed, able to provide different walking abilities in various scenarios. For instance, while stability on level grounds is generally ensured, it is not on unstructured terrains with a consequent poor locomotion performance. Therefore, there is still room for improvement in the design of those devices for an overall locomotion enhancement.
Three key subtopics will be covered during the workshop: (1) design and performance of robotic feet for biped robots, (2) challenges and innovations in prosthetic foot design, and (3) the use of computational modelling techniques for understanding human foot functionality and developing artificial feet. The biomechanical advantages of the human foot's structure will be highlighted as the starting point for robotic and prosthetic feet design. By bringing together experts in biomechanics, robotics, prosthetics, and computational modelling, with this workshop we will facilitate interdisciplinary knowledge exchange and foster innovative solutions to enhance robotic and prosthetic locomotion in real-world scenarios.
